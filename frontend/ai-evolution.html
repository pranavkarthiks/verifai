What actually improved?

Skin & texture

Plastic sheen and waxy skin gave way to pores, uneven tone, and light that sits on the face instead of floating above it.

Eyes & gaze

Dead, mismatched eyes evolved into believable direction, catchlights, and micro-movements — the first place many people still look.

Edges & blend

Harsh halos around hair and jawlines softened; composites sit in the scene instead of looking pasted on.

Lighting match

Faces used to ignore the room; now shadows, colour temperature, and reflections follow the environment more convincingly.

Motion (photo vs video)

In video, jitter, lip slip, and odd blinking were giveaways — each generation smooths those tells, so stills and clips both need a second look.

What actually improved? (voice & scams overall)

Voice cloning

AI learned how to copy tone, accent, and emotion — not just words.

Speech timing

Pauses, hesitation, and urgency now sound natural — not staged.

Personalisation

Scams can reference real names, events, and relationships you recognise.

Speed

What once took months of expert work now takes minutes on a laptop.

Acceleration

This didn’t take decades.

It took years to get started — then only a short span to look and sound realistic. Improvements can now outpace how often people refresh their mental picture of what “fake” looks like.

Early slow progress, then a sharp climb after roughly 2020 — that is when scams started sounding and looking “too real” far more often.

Vision

Seeing is no longer believing.

AI images used to look wrong in obvious ways. Now they can look completely real — even when they aren’t.

Small details still give synthetic media away — but only if you know where to look. Training your eye is part of the same game as training your ear.

Closing

Why this matters

Scams didn’t suddenly appear — they evolved. And that evolution happened in a much shorter window than many people assume.

Today you’re not just spotting bad grammar or strange emails. You’re judging voices, faces, and emotions — and sometimes they look and sound real.

The technology improved. Now your awareness has to catch up.
